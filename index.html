Overview of Central Expressway Section III


       In the Sri Lanka National Physical Plan 2011-2030, five designated regions (referred to as metro regions) have been identified for concentrated development. Additionally, plans are in place to develop nine metro cities within these regions. The North Central metro region, encompassing Anuradhapura. Trincomalee, Polonnaruwa, and Dambulla metro cities, stands out as the largest among them. It is anticipated that populations residing in fragile areas will gradually migrate to these proposed metro regions in the future. Accordingly, the plan outlines the maintenance and enhancement of the existing road network to meet acceptable standards, while also laying the groundwork for connecting metro regions through an expressway network in the future.

       The Central Expressway, cutting through the western, North western, and Central provinces, serves to bolster regional connectivity, and provide improved access to the central hill region. It aims to alleviate traffic congestion on heavily traversed trunk roads, notably the Colombo-Kandy Road (A001) and Ambepussa – Kurunegala – Trincomalee road (A006).

       Moreover, this expressway project is expected to bolster the tourism industry by offering convenient and rapid access to popular tourist destinations. An economic feasibility study conducted by the University of Moratuwa in 2016 evaluated the entire expressway project. According to the study, the Benefit-Cost Ratio (B/C ration) for the entire expressway stands at 1.41, with an Economic Internal Rate of Return (EIRR) of 9.00% at a discounted rate of 7%
