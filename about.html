CENTRAL EXPRESSWAY HISTORY



     Sri Lanka's National Master Plan (2007-2017) prioritizes the Central Expressway for infrastructure development, recognizing the critical role of efficient transportation in economic progress. The government aims to enhance existing road and rail networks, emphasizing the need for an interconnected expressway system. Led by the Ministry of Higher Education and Highways, the Road Development Authority has designed operational segments like the Southern Expressway and the Colombo-Katunayake Expressway. The Central Expressway project, currently in planning, intends to bolster national connectivity, with phases targeting routes from Kadawatha to Dambulla and linking to Kandy.

     Under the Ministry of Higher Education and Highways, the Road Development Authority is conducting a study to establish a suitable route for the Central Expressway Project (CEP), connecting Kadawatha to Dambulla via Kurunegala in phase 1, with a subsequent link to Kandy. Phase 2 aims to extend the expressway to the Northern and Eastern regions. Phase 1 begins at Kadawatha, branching from the Kadawatha-Kerawalapitiya section of the Outer Circular Highway. The route traverses Gampaha, Meerigama, and Kurunegala before reaching Dambulla. A separate branch to Kandy starts at Pothuhera, ending near Galagedara, approximately 10km from Kandy City.

Our Mission

"To provide a high-speed, safe, and comfortable expressway system with minimal accessibilities, ensuring efficient and uninterrupted travel for all users."






The main objectives of the proposed expressway

  • Development of the Northern part of the country including the North - Western, Nothern - Central, Central, Sabaragamuwa, Nothern and Eastern Provinces.


  • Improvement of the mobility between Northern and Eastern Provinces.


  • As asolution for the traffic congestion on Colombo - Kandy Road (A001 Road) and Ambepussa - Kurunagala - Trincomalee Road (A006 Road) of which alignments cannot be improved further to suit the travel speed required for the future traffic conditions.


  • Improvement of the efficiency of the transport system.


  • Improvement of the quality of the transport system including the safe environment.


  • Reduction of air pollution due to traffic congestion and unnecessary combustion of fossil fuel.


  • Elimination of restrictions to travel speed through a limited access expressway.


  • Improvement of inter provincial connectivity. This expressway directly connects with Colombo Outer Circular Highway (E002), Colombo - Katunayaka Expressway (E003), Colombo - Kandy Road (A001 Road), Katugastota - Kurunagala - Puttalam Road (A010) and many other National Highways.


  • Improvement of the social and economic condition of areas stated and the reduction of poverty of the people in general.
